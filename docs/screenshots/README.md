### Lo-res colour: why the gallery shot is hand-captured from Mariani

`graphics.png` is grabbed by hand from the **Mariani** emulator, *not* by
`make screenshots`. izapple2's `headless` only emits
**NTSC colour** (`png`) — soft by design, because it extracts colour from a
monochrome NTSC signal — or crisp **monochrome** (`pngm`); it has no crisp-RGB
mode for lo-res. Mariani's RGB-monitor render gives the crisp colour blocks, so
`make screenshots` deliberately **skips** this file and never clobbers it.
(`make screenshots SHOTS=graphics` regenerates the softer izapple2 version if you
want it.)

`colorbars.png` is also a manual Mariani capture; use the `colorbars` recipe
below when you want to refresh it.

`snake.gif` **is** tool-generated (`make screenshots SHOTS=snake`): the capture
script drives the game one frame at a time, snapshots each PNG, and stitches them
with ffmpeg. The NTSC softness barely shows on the simple green-on-black trail,
so it stays in the tool rather than needing a Mariani capture.

`grdemo.gif` is also tool-generated (`make screenshots SHOTS=grdemo`): it boots
the flat II+ Family B **compiler** disk with the **data disk** in drive 2,
`[X]`-runs the oversize `XGRDEMO.SWIFT` off it, and samples the five auto-advancing
scenes (colour bars, concentric squares, a starfield, a bouncing ball, a rainbow
flash) into a sped-up loop. Its NTSC colour is the soft `headless` render — a
moving showcase reads fine animated, where a crisp single still still wants
Mariani (`graphics.png` / `colorbars.png`).

**To (re)capture a lo-res shot from Mariani:**

1. `make run-mari-sat` — boots the II+ Saturn REPL disk in Mariani.
2. **`graphics`:** at the launcher press **2** (File selector) → **Return** to
   open the volume → step to `/XSAMPLES`, press **.** to enter → highlight
   `XGRAPHICS.SWIFT` → press **X** to run; the picture stays up at "press return".
3. **`colorbars`:** same as `graphics`, on the shipped
   [`xcolors`](../../progdisk/xsamples/xcolors.swift) sample — press **2**
   (File selector) → **Return** → step to `/XSAMPLES`, press **.** to enter →
   highlight `XCOLORS.SWIFT` → press **X** to run. The bands fill the screen
   and it waits at "press return".
   > Run it from the **browser** (`X`), *not* the editor's **Ctrl-R**: on a
   > Saturn REPL disk Ctrl-R runs the file through a path that doesn't see the
   > extras graphics builtins, so `gr`/`color`/`vlin` come back as
   > `undeclared name`. `[X]` from the browser runs it with SWIFTSAT, where the
   > graphics builtins live.
4. Capture just the screen with **Shift-Command-4** and drag a box over the
   Apple II display (skip the title bar).
5. Save/move the PNG to the matching gallery path. For `graphics`, use
   `docs/screenshots/graphics.png`; for `colorbars`, use
   `docs/screenshots/colorbars.png`.

## Photos & video worth taking on the real Apple II+

The emulator captures the *screen*; nothing beats real iron for showing that
SwiftII boots and runs on a 1 MHz 6502 from 1979. Suggestions, roughly in order
of impact:

**Hero stills**
- ✅ The **whole setup** powered on at the REPL — Apple II+, monitor, Disk II
  drive(s), the SwiftII floppy half-inserted or labelled — in one frame. This is
  the shot that says "this is real." *(Captured: [`hero-apple2plus.jpg`](hero-apple2plus.jpg), now the README cover.)*
- A **close-up of the monitor** at the `SwiftII ][+` banner and the
  `the answer is 42` one-liner, so it pairs with `repl-hero.png`.
- The **lo-res colour graphics** (`xgraphics` / `xsnake`) on the real monitor —
  the single most worthwhile real-hardware shot, because the emulator's colour is
  the weakest part of the capture.
- A **labelled SwiftII floppy** in hand or in the drive.

**Short video clips (15–45 s each)** — these carry far more than stills:
- **Cold boot → launcher**: power on (or `C600G` / `PR#6` on an original ][),
  the drive chattering, ProDOS loading, landing in the launcher menu.
- **A live REPL session**: typing `let`/`print`, an array, an optional — the
  per-keystroke feel on the real keyboard (and the II+ uppercase input tricks:
  `'` for a capital, the digraphs).
- **Run a sample from the browser**: pick `xgraphics` or `xsnake`, watch it draw;
  for `xsnake`, actually *play* it with I/J/K/M — motion sells it.
- **The speaker demo** (`xspeaker` / a `tone()` program): point the mic at the
  machine so the **sound** is on the soundtrack — the click/beep coming out of
  real hardware is something no screenshot conveys.
- **The compile-then-run flow** on a Family B disk: `[X]` a `.swift`, the
  `COMPILING…` → `RUNNING…` banners, the disk seeking between the compiler and
  runner passes.
- **The editor**: open a sample, type a line, Ctrl-R to run it — the
  write/save/run loop entirely on the floppy.

**Tips for clean captures**
- A direct **composite/RGB feed** (or a good capture card) beats a phone pointed
  at a CRT; if you must shoot the CRT, kill room glare and match shutter to the
  refresh to avoid scan-line banding.
- Show the **drive light** and, where you can, **disk-swap moments** — they make
  the "boots from a 140 KB floppy" claim concrete.
- For original-][ owners, film the **`C600G` from the monitor prompt** — it's a
  charming detail that proves the no-Autostart machine is supported.
